## Overview

This playbook provides a comprehensive guide to the **IRS Form 1040** for Tax Year 2025, covering all filing requirements, income types, deductions, credits, and special situations. It incorporates inflation-adjusted figures published by the IRS for 2025.

**Governing Law:** Internal Revenue Code (IRC); IRS Publication 17; Instructions for Form 1040 (2025)
**Filing Deadline:** April 15, 2026 (October 15, 2026 with extension — Form 4868)
**E-file Requirement Threshold:** IRS recommends e-filing; paid preparers with 11+ returns must e-file

## Income — All Categories

### Wages, Salaries, and Tips (W-2 Income)
- Box 1 of W-2; includes bonuses, awards, severance
- Employer-provided housing, meals exceeding exclusion limits
- Imputed income (GTL over $50,000, certain fringe benefits)

### Interest Income (Schedule B)
- **Ordinary interest:** Fully taxable (1099-INT, Box 1)
- **Tax-exempt interest:** Municipal bonds — not in taxable income but reported on Line 2a
- **U.S. Savings Bond interest:** Taxable at federal level; excludable at state level
- OID (Original Issue Discount) reported on 1099-OID

### Dividend Income (Schedule B)
- **Ordinary dividends:** Taxed as ordinary income (1099-DIV, Box 1a)
- **Qualified dividends:** Taxed at preferential LTCG rates (1099-DIV, Box 1b)
- **Capital gain distributions:** Mutual fund distributions (reported as LTCG)
- Threshold for Schedule B: Interest or dividends > $1,500

### Business Income (Schedule C)
- Net profit/loss from sole proprietorship or single-member LLC
- SE tax net earnings × 92.35% = SE income
- Subject to self-employment tax (Schedule SE) + regular income tax
- QBI deduction (Section 199A) may apply: up to 20% of qualified business income

### Capital Gains and Losses (Schedule D + Form 8949)
**Holding Periods:**
- **Short-term:** Held ≤12 months → taxed as ordinary income
- **Long-term:** Held >12 months → preferential rates

**2025 Long-Term Capital Gains Tax Rates:**
| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|---|---|---|
| 0% | $0 – $48,350 | $0 – $96,700 |
| 15% | $48,351 – $533,400 | $96,701 – $600,050 |
| 20% | >$533,400 | >$600,050 |

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT):** 3.8% on lesser of NII or MAGI above threshold ($200K single / $250K MFJ)

**Capital Loss Limitation:** Up to $3,000/year deductible against ordinary income; excess carries forward
**Wash Sale Rule:** Loss disallowed if substantially identical security purchased within 30 days before/after sale

### IRA and Retirement Distributions (Form 1099-R)
- **Traditional IRA / 401(k):** Distributions fully taxable (if deductions were taken)
- **Roth IRA:** Qualified distributions tax-free; non-qualified distributions subject to ordering rules
- **Early Distribution Penalty:** 10% additional tax if under age 59½ (exceptions apply)
- **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs):** Starting age 73 (SECURE 2.0); RMDs not eligible for rollover

### Social Security Benefits
- If only income → generally not taxable
- Combined income (AGI + nontaxable interest + 50% of SS benefits) thresholds:
  - Single: $25,000–$34,000 → 50% taxable; >$34,000 → 85% taxable
  - MFJ: $32,000–$44,000 → 50% taxable; >$44,000 → 85% taxable

### Other Income Types
| Income Type | Form/Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Alimony (pre-2019 divorces) | Divorce decree | Taxable to recipient; deductible by payer |
| Gambling winnings | W-2G or cash | Fully taxable; losses deductible only to extent of winnings (itemized) |
| Rental income | Schedule E | Net income after allowable deductions |
| Partnership/S-Corp income | Schedule K-1 | Passthrough income/loss |
| Royalties | Schedule E | Oil/gas, copyright, patents |

| Unemployment Compensation | 1099-G | Fully taxable |
| Foreign Income | Form 2555/1116 | Exclusion or credit available for U.S. expatriates |

## Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)

Claimed when total itemized deductions exceed standard deduction.

### State and Local Taxes (SALT) — Capped at $10,000
- State/local income taxes OR general sales taxes (not both)
- Real property taxes
- Personal property taxes (ad valorem basis)

### Mortgage Interest (Form 1098)
- Acquisition debt: Interest on up to $750,000 of home acquisition debt (MFJ; $375,000 MFS)
- Grandfathered debt before Dec 15, 2017: $1,000,000 limit
- Home equity loan interest: Deductible only if proceeds used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home

### Charitable Contributions
- Cash: Substantiated by canceled check or written receipt; $250+ requires written acknowledgment
- Noncash under $250: Written record
- Noncash $250–$500: Written contemporaneous acknowledgment
- Noncash $500–$5,000: Form 8283; written acknowledgment
- Noncash >$5,000: Qualified appraisal required; Form 8283
- **AGI Limits:** Cash to public charity — 60% AGI; capital gain property — 30% AGI; carryover 5 years
- **QCD (Qualified Charitable Distribution):** Ages 70½+; up to $105,000 directly from IRA; excludes from income

### Medical Expenses
- Deductible portion: Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding **7.5% of AGI**
- Eligible: Doctor/dental/vision fees, prescriptions, long-term care premiums (limits by age), health insurance if not pre-tax

### Casualty and Theft Losses
- Only federally declared disaster areas (TCJA through 2025)
- Loss = FMV before − FMV after (or basis, whichever lower) minus insurance recovery; minus $100; minus 10% AGI

### Miscellaneous Itemized Deductions
- Subject to 2% AGI floor — SUSPENDED through 2025 (TCJA)
- Gambling losses (not subject to 2% floor — deductible to extent of winnings)

## Self-Employment and Business Taxes

### Self-Employment Tax (Schedule SE)
- **Rate:** 15.3% on first $176,100 of net SE income (2025 SS wage base); 2.9% on excess
- **Deduction:** 50% of SE tax deductible above-the-line

### Qualified Business Income Deduction (§ 199A)
- Up to **20%** of qualified business income (QBI) from pass-through entities
- **W-2 wage limitation** applies above income thresholds: $197,300 (single) / $394,600 (MFJ) in 2025
- **Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs):** Health, law, consulting, finance, accounting, performing arts — limited/phased out above income thresholds

### Net Operating Loss (NOL)
- Post-2017: NOL carryforward indefinitely; limited to 80% of taxable income per year
- No carryback (except farming losses — 2-year carryback available)

## Special Situations

### Kiddie Tax (Form 8615)
- Applies to children under 19 (under 24 if full-time student) with unearned income > $2,600
- Unearned income above $2,600 taxed at parent's marginal rate

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
- 3.8% tax on lesser of: NII OR MAGI above threshold ($200,000 single / $250,000 MFJ)
- NII: interest, dividends, capital gains, rental income, passive business income

### Additional Medicare Tax
- 0.9% on wages/SE income above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ)

### Foreign Accounts / FBAR
- If foreign account > $10,000 at any time during year → FinCEN 114 required separately
- Form 8938 (FATCA): Assets > $50,000 (single) / $100,000 (MFJ) at year end

### Virtual Currency
- Every disposition (sale, exchange, payment) of virtual currency is a taxable event
- Mining/staking income is ordinary income on date received at FMV
- Hard forks creating new tokens: ordinary income at FMV received

| Action | Deadline | Form |

| File return | April 15, 2026 | Form 1040 |
| File for extension | April 15, 2026 | Form 4868 |
| Extended filing deadline | October 15, 2026 | — |

**Underpayment Penalty:** Applies if tax withheld + estimated payments < 90% of 2025 tax OR 100% of 2024 tax (110% if 2024 AGI > $150,000) — Safe Harbor.

**Late Filing Penalty:** 5% per month (up to 25%) of unpaid tax
**Late Payment Penalty:** 0.5% per month (up to 25%) of unpaid tax
**Interest:** Federal short-term rate + 3%, compounded daily
