# FMLA Compliance Playbook
**Domain:** Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
**Version:** 1
**Owner:** Bree AI — HR & Employment Law

---

## Overview

This playbook governs employer compliance with the **Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (FMLA)**, 29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq., and its implementing regulations at **29 CFR Part 825**.

FMLA entitles eligible employees of covered employers to take up to **12 weeks** (or **26 weeks** for military caregiver leave) of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for specified family and medical reasons.

**Key Enforcement:** U.S. Department of Labor, Wage and Hour Division (WHD)
**Private right of action:** Employees may sue for interference or retaliation (29 U.S.C. § 2615)

---

## Domain Entities

### Covered Employer
An employer subject to FMLA obligations.
- **Private sector:** 50+ employees within 75 miles of the employee's worksite, for 20+ workweeks in current or preceding calendar year
- **Public agencies:** All public agencies (federal, state, local) regardless of size
- **Schools:** All public and private elementary/secondary schools regardless of size

### Eligible Employee
An employee who meets all three criteria:
1. Employed by a covered employer
2. Worked for the employer for **at least 12 months** (need not be consecutive)
3. Worked at least **1,250 hours** in the 12 months immediately preceding the leave start date
4. Works at a location where the employer has **50+ employees within 75 miles**

### FMLA Year (12-Month Period)
The 12-month period used to measure the employee's 12-week entitlement. Employers must select and consistently apply one of four methods:
1. **Calendar year** (Jan 1 – Dec 31)
2. **Fixed leave year** (any fixed 12-month period, e.g., fiscal year)
3. **Rolling forward:** 12-month period measured forward from the first date leave is taken
4. **Rolling backward (most employer-protective):** 12-month period measured backward from the date leave is used

### Qualifying Reason for FMLA Leave
| Reason | Leave Entitlement |
|---|---|
| Birth of child and bonding | 12 weeks |
| Adoption or foster care placement + bonding | 12 weeks |
| Serious health condition of spouse, child, or parent | 12 weeks |
| Employee's own serious health condition | 12 weeks |
| Qualifying military exigency | 12 weeks |
| Military caregiver (covered servicemember) | 26 weeks |
| Military caregiver (covered veteran) | 26 weeks |

### Serious Health Condition (SHC)
An illness, injury, impairment, or physical or mental condition that involves:
1. **Inpatient care** — any period of incapacity requiring overnight stay in hospital, hospice, or residential medical facility, or any subsequent treatment following inpatient care
2. **Continuing treatment** — incapacity for 3+ consecutive calendar days PLUS:
   - Two or more visits to a health care provider within 30 days of onset, OR
   - First visit within 7 days + a course of continuing treatment
3. **Pregnancy/prenatal care** — any period of incapacity due to pregnancy
4. **Chronic SHC** — requiring periodic visits (≥2/year) to a health care provider; may cause episodic incapacity
5. **Permanent/long-term SHC** — ongoing incapacity (e.g., Alzheimer's, severe stroke)
6. **Multiple treatments** — conditions requiring multiple treatments (chemotherapy, dialysis, physical therapy)

### Intermittent Leave
FMLA leave taken in separate blocks of time or by reducing the normal weekly/daily work schedule.
- May be taken in increments as small as **1 hour** (or the employer's shortest increment for other leaves)
- Foreseeable intermittent leave requires employee to schedule to minimize disruption
- Employer may transfer employee to an equivalent position better suited to intermittent leave

### Key Personnel
Employer may deny FMLA restoration to a "key employee" if:
- Salaried employee among top 10% of earners within 75 miles, AND
- Denial is necessary to prevent substantial and grievous economic injury to operations
- Must notify employee of "key employee" status and injury determination

## Employer Notice Obligations

### General Notice (Poster)
- FMLA poster must be displayed prominently in all workplaces
- **Penalty:** Up to $226 per willful violation

### Written Policy Notice
If employer has a written FMLA policy, must provide to employees at hire or upon request.

### Eligibility Notice (Form WH-381)
- Must be provided within **5 business days** of employer learning of potential FMLA leave need
- Must state whether employee is eligible or, if not, primary reason for ineligibility
- Must provide Rights & Responsibilities Notice simultaneously

### Rights & Responsibilities Notice (Form WH-381)
Provided with Eligibility Notice. Must inform employee of:
- Specific expectations/obligations during leave
- Consequences of failing to meet obligations
- Whether leave will be designated as FMLA
- Whether employer requires substitution of paid leave
- Whether medical certification is required

### Designation Notice (Form WH-382)
- Must be provided within **5 business days** after employer has sufficient information to determine whether leave qualifies as FMLA
- States whether leave is: Approved FMLA / Pending (certification needed) / Not FMLA-qualifying
- If approved, states amount of leave counted against the 12-week entitlement

## Medical Certification

### When Required
Employer may require medical certification when leave is for a serious health condition of employee or family member.

### Certification Timeline
- Employee has **15 calendar days** to return completed certification
- Employer may grant extension if employee cannot return in 15 days despite diligent effort

### Certification Content (Form WH-380-E / WH-380-F)
- Identity of healthcare provider
- Approximate date condition began; probable duration
- Whether the condition meets FMLA SHC criteria
- For intermittent leave: frequency and duration of expected absences

### Authentication / Clarification
- Employer may contact healthcare provider (through HR, not direct supervisor) only to authenticate or clarify — NOT to gather additional information
- **May NOT require second opinion** for initial certification — but can for fitness-for-duty

### Recertification
- Generally permitted no more often than every **30 days** in connection with an absence
- Can request recertification every **6 months** for long-term conditions regardless of absences
- Can always request if: circumstances change significantly, employer receives information casting doubt on stated reason

### Fitness-for-Duty Certification
- Employer may require prior to reinstatement for employee's own SHC
- Must notify employee of requirement in the Designation Notice
- Can only cover the particular SHC that caused leave; cannot be a general exam

## Leave Designation and Counting

### Employer's Duty to Designate
- Employer **must** designate leave as FMLA-qualifying when it has sufficient information — even if employee has not requested FMLA leave
- Cannot retroactively designate leave if the failure to do so harms the employee
- Late designation is permissible only if employee is not harmed

### Substitution of Paid Leave
- Employer may require (or employee may elect) to substitute accrued paid leave (vacation, sick, PTO) to run concurrently with FMLA leave
- Employer's paid leave policies apply for determining which categories of paid leave may be substituted

### Leave Without Pay (LWOP)
- Employer does not pay salary during FMLA leave unless paid leave is substituted
- Benefits cost continuation may be required; employer can recover premium if employee does not return

---

## Benefit Continuation During FMLA

### Group Health Insurance
Employer **must** maintain group health insurance coverage during FMLA leave on the same terms as if employee had continued working.
- Employee must continue paying their share of premiums (or reimburse employer if not paid)
- If employee fails to return after FMLA (for reasons other than continuation of SHC or circumstances beyond employee's control):
  - Employer may recover premiums paid during leave

### Other Benefits
- Seniority and other benefits do NOT need to accrue during leave unless employer provides the same for other types of leaves
- Life, disability and other non-health benefits: governed by employer's established policy

## Reinstatement Rights

### Same or Equivalent Position
Upon return from FMLA leave, employee is entitled to:
- The **same position**, OR
- An **equivalent position**: Same pay, benefits, work schedule, working conditions, and substantially similar duties and responsibilities

### No Greater Rights
FMLA does not entitle employee to greater rights than they would have had absent the leave (e.g., employee can still be laid off during leave if the layoff would have occurred anyway).

### Key Employee Exception
High-paid key employees may be denied reinstatement only if all procedural steps are followed.

## Anti-Retaliation / Interference

### Prohibited Conduct (29 U.S.C. § 2615)
- **Interference:** Restraining, denying, or interfering with FMLA rights
- **Retaliation:** Discharge or discrimination against employee for exercising FMLA rights

### Common Violations
- Terminating employee on or shortly after FMLA leave
- Denying or discouraging use of FMLA
- Failing to notify employee of FMLA eligibility
- Changing terms/conditions of employment after FMLA request
- Using FMLA leave as a negative factor in performance reviews

## Military FMLA Leave (Special Rules)

### Qualifying Exigency (12 weeks)
Leave for covered military member's: short-notice deployment, military events, childcare/school, financial/legal, counseling, rest and recuperation (up to 15 days), post-deployment activities.

### Military Caregiver Leave (26 weeks)
For employee to care for covered servicemember (current) or veteran (discharged ≤5 years) with serious injury or illness incurred in or aggravated by military service.
- Only **once per servicemember per injury** (not per year)
- Healthcare provider for servicemember may include DOD/VA providers

## Key Deadlines Summary

| Notice/Action | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Eligibility Notice to employee | 5 business days of learning of leave need |
| Rights & Responsibilities Notice | Concurrent with Eligibility Notice |
| Designation Notice | 5 business days after sufficient info to determine qualifying |
| Employee medical certification return | 15 calendar days |
| Recertification interval | Not more often than every 30 days (with absence) |
| Retroactive designation | Permissible if employee not harmed |
| Return from leave — reinstatement right | Immediately upon return |
