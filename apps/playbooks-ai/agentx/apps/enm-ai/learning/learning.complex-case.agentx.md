# Case Study: 60-Year-Old Female, Complex Abdominal & Pulmonary Comorbidities

## Patient Orientation
- **Age/Sex**: 60-year-old Female
- **Status**: Post-acute recovery, recently discharged to SnF/Rehab (Rockledge Health and Rehab)
- **Primary History**: Complicated abdominal issues, perforated viscus s/p partial colectomy x 2, ESBL MDRO E. coli, VRE, and Candida glabrata peritonitis.

## Timeline & Encounter History

### Pre-Rehab Hospitalization (4/9 - 5/1/2025)
- **Location**: Holmes Regional Medical Center
- **Treatment**: 10 days Meropenem/Linezolid for ESBL E. Coli, VRE, Candida peritonitis.
- **Outcome**: Sent to Rehab on 5/1.

### Acute Readmission (Post 5/1)
- **Presentation**: Hypoxia (SpO2 27%), tachycardia, leukocytosis, lactic acidosis (3).
- **Findings**: CTA chest showed emphysema (no PE). CT abdomen showed peripancreatic fluid collection (2.7 x 1.5 cm) + occluded splenic artery/vein with 80% splenic infarct.
- **Discharge**: May 10, 2025 to complete antibiotics.

### Specialist Follow-ups (Rehab Visits)

#### 5/17 - Second Visit
- **Subjective**: Patient refusing nightly antibiotics (wakes her up). Complains of diffuse body ache and mild abdominal pain.
- **Objective**: Colostomy has brown stool.
- **Plan**: Prolonged discussion on drug-resistant infection risks. Agreed to q6h infusion. Discussed stopping statin (patient feels unwell on it). Discussed vaccinations (PCV21, MenACWY, Hib).

#### 5/20 - Follow-up
- **Subjective**: Quite weak, intake slowly improving. No acute complaints.
- **Objective**: Appears slightly dehydrated. Tolerating diet but struggling with weight gain.

#### 5/29 - Follow-up
- **Subjective**: Feels remarkably well. Tolerating diet + PT.
- **Requests**: Increase Creon to 2 tabs/meal due to stoma pain.

#### 5/31 - Acute Incident
- **Status**: Acute respiratory distress.
- **Findings**: Tripod position, pursed lip breathing, diffuse wheezing.
- **Action**: Emergent transfer to hospital for Acute COPD Exacerbation.
- **Gi Concern**: Increased ostomy output (500-600cc, 3-4x/day).
