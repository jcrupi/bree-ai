# Pain AI — Medical Rules & Codes Playbook

> **Purpose:** Clinical rules and codes for pain management procedures, prior authorization, and documentation. Aligned with CMS LCD/NCD, AMA CPT®, and payer policies.

**Sources:** AMA CPT®, CMS LCD L38958 (Pain Management), CMS LCD L38959 (Epidural Injections), CMS LCD L38960 (Facet Joint Injections), ICD-10-CM

---

## 1. Definition and Scope

### 1.1 Pain Management Coding

Pain management coding covers:

- **Interventional procedures:** Epidurals, facet injections, nerve blocks, radiofrequency ablation (RFA), spinal cord stimulation
- **CPT range:** 62310–64636 (injections), 63650–63664 (neurostimulators), 64400–64636 (nerve blocks)
- **Prior authorization:** Many procedures require PA per payer policy
- **Documentation:** Must support medical necessity, failed conservative care, imaging correlation

### 1.2 CMS Alignment

- LCDs define medical necessity criteria for epidurals, facet injections, RFA
- NCCI edits apply to multiple injections, E/M + procedure same-day
- Documentation must support: diagnosis, failed conservative treatment, imaging findings, level(s) treated

## 4. Documentation Requirements

### 4.1 Required for All Procedures

- **Chief complaint:** Pain location, duration, character
- **History:** Prior treatments (PT, medications, injections), response
- **Physical exam:** Relevant neurologic, musculoskeletal findings
- **Imaging:** MRI or CT supporting diagnosis and level(s)
- **Diagnosis:** ICD-10 supporting medical necessity
- **Procedure note:** Level(s), approach, imaging guidance, medications

### 4.2 Failed Conservative Care

Documentation must show ≥4 weeks of one or more:

- Physical therapy
- Home exercise program
- NSAIDs or other oral analgesics
- Activity modification

### 4.3 Facet/RFA-Specific

- **Diagnostic blocks:** Positive response (≥50% pain relief) before RFA
- **Level correlation:** Imaging must correlate with clinical level
- **Repeat RFA:** Typically not before 6–12 months; document rationale if sooner

### 4.4 Epidural-Specific

- **Radiculopathy:** Document radicular symptoms, positive imaging
- **Level:** Inject at symptomatic level(s)
- **Frequency:** Typically limited (e.g., 2–4 per year per LCD)

## 5. Prior Authorization

### 5.1 Procedures Often Requiring PA

- Epidural injections (interlaminar, transforaminal)
- Facet joint injections (beyond initial diagnostic)
- Radiofrequency ablation
- Spinal cord stimulation
- Intrathecal pumps

### 5.2 PA Documentation Bundle

- Clinical note with history, exam, imaging review
- Failed conservative care summary
- Imaging report (MRI/CT) with date
- Prior injection response (if applicable)
- Requested procedure and level(s)

## 6. NCCI and Modifier Rules

### 6.1 Same-Day E/M + Procedure

- **Modifier 25:** E/M separately identifiable from procedure
- Document distinct E/M service (e.g., new problem, significant work beyond procedure)
- **E/M level:** Use MDM or Time (whichever supports level). Two of three MDM elements (Problems, Data, Risk) must meet level. See `enm-ai.playbook.agentx-v1.md` Sections 3–3.7 for full MDM tables and clinical examples.

### 6.2 Bilateral Procedures

- **Modifier 50:** Bilateral procedure
- Or report with RT/LT modifiers per payer preference

### 6.3 Multiple Levels

- Report add-on codes (64491, 64492, etc.) for additional levels
- Do not unbundle; follow NCCI column 2 edits

### 6.4 Imaging Guidance

- **Fluoroscopy:** 77002 (spine) or 77003 when not included in injection code
- **Ultrasound:** 76942 when used
- Check NCCI for bundling with injection codes

## 9. Chart Validity — Pain Management Signals

A document is a **valid pain procedure encounter** when it contains ≥2 of:

- Chief complaint (pain location, duration, character)
- Prior treatments (PT, medications, injections) and response
- Imaging (MRI/CT) with date and findings
- Diagnosis (ICD-10) supporting procedure
- Procedure note (level(s), approach, imaging guidance)

**Reject** if: no pain-related diagnosis, no imaging when required, or non-pain content.

---

## 10. Contradiction Detection

**Flag** when:

- RFA requested without documented positive diagnostic block
- Imaging older than 12 months when required
- Failed conservative care not documented (≥4 weeks)
- CPT–ICD mismatch (e.g., facet code with radiculopathy-only diagnosis)
- Frequency limits exceeded (e.g., epidurals >4/year per LCD)
